Time Well Spent
Volunteer App
Case Study

Role: UX Designer
Team: Kara Page, Rachel Eisner and Daniel Venter
Tools: InVision, Figma, Miro
Timeline: 3 weeks

Project Description: Our team joined together to create an app that would help the homeless community. Our goal was to connect organizations and volunteers while finding a solution that would streamline the volunteering process. The app will help to create more awareness and give volunteers more opportunities to give their time and resources

The Research

To begin our research, we mapped out and discussed internally who our user might be, as we started to build our proto persona.

Next, we conducted an online survey and remote interviews with volunteers. We began to focus on our target audience who we named “Gabby Give Back.”

A user persona was then created based on a volunteer audience and potential pain points they might endure during their volunteer experiences.

We concluded that awareness of volunteer opportunities and finding the time to volunteer were the biggest challenges our persona faced.

Interview Plan

Next, we contacted homeless organizations, sought out volunteers, and conducted interviews to provide insights into their experiences when volunteering. The interviews were given to five users based on 25 to 45 years of age and volunteer frequency were identified to conduct a planned survey interviewed and recorded over Zoom. Once the interviews were complete, we did a competitive analysis to look at similar volunteer apps to explore strengths and weaknesses.
